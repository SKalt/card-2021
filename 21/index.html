Recommended:

Ship of Theseus

by J. J. Abrams

Most of the stories happens in multiple layers of notes scribbled in the margins, which makes for an inventive mystery left to the reader. This books is Emma’s. —Steven

This book came recommended to me by one of my best friends in medical school. I considered him one of my “well read” friends since he was part of a book club composed of him and one other person. I asked for a book recommendation and he asked “how weird do you want?”

The book is multiple stories within one. There are numerous debates online about how one is supposed to read this book. I kind of did a mix of all the recommendations I could find. Try it out! —Emma

Recommended:

Pride and Prejudice

by Jane Austen

A classic, of course. You’ll see that we have multiple copies. I first watched the movie sophomore year of college and loved the story and found Mr. Bennett utterly hilarious. I read the book for the first time the following year for my English class. Since then, I have collected multiple printings, all used of course, and listened to it on audiobook while doing tissue culture in the lab. A nice story about a strong-willed lady. — Emma
